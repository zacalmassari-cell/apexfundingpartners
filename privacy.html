1. Who we are

Apex Funding Partners is a UK grant consultancy trading as a sole trader. We help businesses and homeowners identify and apply for UK government grants and R&D tax relief.

Data controller: Zacharia Malik Al-Massari, trading as Apex Funding Partners
Website: apexfundingpartners.com
Email: hello@apexfundingpartners.com
Country of operation: United Kingdom

As the data controller, we are responsible for deciding how and why your personal data is processed. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and what your rights are under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

We are required to register with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller if we process personal data. Our ICO registration number will be displayed here once registration is complete.

2. What personal data we collect

We collect personal data in the following ways:

When you contact us or book a call

  • Your name and email address
  • Your phone number (if provided)
  • The content of any message you send us
  • Appointment booking data via Calendly (name, email, meeting preferences)

When you complete our client intake form or onboarding

  • Name, email address, phone number, and job title
  • Company name, Companies House number, website, and sector
  • Financial information including turnover ranges, staff costs, and profitability status
  • Information about your R&D activities and technical projects
  • Accountant contact details
  • For homeowner grant enquiries: postcode, property type, benefits received, and EPC rating

When you make a payment

  • Billing name and email address
  • Payment is processed securely by Stripe. We do not store card details — these are handled entirely by Stripe and subject to their privacy policy.

Automatically when you visit our website

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site
  • Referring website or search query
  • Device type and operating system
  • This data is collected via Google Analytics cookies (see Section 7)

3. How we use your data

We use your personal data only for the purposes described below:

  • To deliver our grant consultancy services — assessing grant eligibility, preparing and submitting applications, and communicating with you about your case
  • To process payments — through our payment processor Stripe
  • To communicate with you — responding to enquiries, sending eligibility reports, and providing updates on applications
  • To schedule calls and meetings — via Calendly
  • To improve our website — using anonymised analytics data from Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our site
  • To comply with legal obligations — including tax and accounting records

We will never sell your personal data to third parties. We will never use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

4. Our legal basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing your personal data. We rely on the following:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Processing is necessary to deliver the services you have engaged us for — including completing intake forms, preparing grant applications, and managing your client account
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): We process website analytics data to improve our site and understand visitor behaviour. Our legitimate interest in running an effective business does not override your privacy rights
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): For non-essential cookies (including Google Analytics), we rely on your consent via our cookie banner
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): We retain certain financial and business records to comply with HMRC and accounting requirements

5. Who we share your data with

We share your data only with third-party service providers who help us deliver our services. All processors are contractually bound to handle your data securely and in accordance with UK GDPR.

Calendly (meeting scheduling) — processes your name and email to book appointments. Privacy policy: calendly.com/privacy

Stripe (payment processing) — processes billing name and email. Card data is held by Stripe only. Privacy policy: stripe.com/gb/privacy

Google Analytics (website analytics) — collects anonymised usage data via cookies. Privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy

Google Workspace (email and document storage) — your emails and intake form data may be stored in Google's servers. Privacy policy: workspace.google.com/terms/privacy.html

Your accountant — where required to submit R&D tax credit claims via your Corporation Tax return, we share your technical narrative and cost schedule with your named accountant only.

We do not share your data with any other third parties unless required to do so by law (for example, in response to a court order or regulatory request).

6. How long we keep your data

  • Client records and application data: 6 years from the end of the client relationship, in line with HMRC record-keeping requirements and the Limitation Act 1980
  • Enquiry and contact data (non-clients): 12 months from last contact, after which it is securely deleted
  • Payment records: 6 years, as required by HMRC
  • Website analytics data: 26 months (Google Analytics default retention period)
  • Calendly booking data: In accordance with Calendly's own retention policy

When data is no longer needed, we delete it securely. You may request earlier deletion — see Section 8 for your rights.

7. Cookies and tracking

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device. We use the following types:

Strictly necessary cookies

These are required for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They do not store any personally identifiable information.

Analytics cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our website. This sets cookies that collect information in an anonymised form, including the number of visitors to the site, where visitors have come from, and which pages they visited.

Google Analytics cookies set: _ga (expires 2 years), _ga_XCMDX3BHTC (expires 2 years)
These cookies are only set if you accept analytics cookies via our cookie banner.

Managing your cookie preferences

When you first visit our site, you will be asked to accept or decline non-essential cookies via our cookie banner. You can change your preferences at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site. You can also control cookies through your browser settings.

For more information about cookies and how to control them, visit allaboutcookies.org or the ICO's guidance at ico.org.uk.

8. Your rights under UK GDPR

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. These rights apply in most circumstances — some have conditions or exceptions.

Right of access
You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a Subject Access Request).
Right to rectification
You can ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold.
Right to erasure
You can ask us to delete your personal data where there is no legitimate reason for us to continue holding it.
Right to restrict processing
You can ask us to pause processing your data in certain circumstances.
Right to data portability
You can request your data in a structured, machine-readable format where we process it by automated means.
Right to object
You can object to processing based on legitimate interests. We must stop unless we can demonstrate compelling grounds.
Right to withdraw consent
Where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time. This does not affect the lawfulness of prior processing.
Right to complain
You have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO if you believe we have breached UK GDPR.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@apexfundingpartners.com. We will respond within one calendar month. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests.

9. How we protect your data

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction. These include:

  • HTTPS encryption on our website (enforced via GitHub Pages)
  • Payment data handled exclusively by Stripe, which is PCI DSS compliant
  • Access to client data restricted to the data controller only
  • Secure, password-protected email and document storage via Google Workspace
  • Regular review of data held to delete what is no longer needed

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and, where required, notify you directly.

10. International data transfers

Some of our service providers (including Google and Stripe) may transfer or store data outside the UK. Where this occurs, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR, including reliance on adequacy decisions or standard contractual clauses.

Calendly and Stripe are both US-based companies. Transfers are made under the UK-US data bridge arrangement or equivalent safeguards.
